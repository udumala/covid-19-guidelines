Prevention Steps

Maintain Physical Distance

Physical distancing measures aim to slow the spread of disease by stopping chains of transmission of the Covid-19 and preventing new ones from appearing. Therefore, always maintain physical distance of at least one metre between people, and avoid unessassary contact with contaminated surfaces.

Wash Your Hands

COVID-19 spreads when droplets containing the virus get into your body through your eyes, nose or mouth. Most often, this happens through your hands. Hands are also one of the most common ways that the virus spreads. Wash your hands as many times a day as you can, especially after going out, before preparing food, eating.

Stay At Home

Following stay-at-home orders reduces the risk even more by removing human contact, except with family members in the home. While there are other factors that impact how the virus spreads, isolating is one of the most important things someone can do to help avoid infection or becoming a carrier.

Use Napkin

By following good respiratory hygiene you protect the people around you from viruses such as cold, flu and COVID-19.So, make sure you, and the people around you, follow good respiratory hygiene. This means covering your mouth and nose with tissue when you cough or sneeze, then dispose it immediately.

Wear a Mask

Mask-wearing is most effective when it's a communal effort. Surgical masks and cloth coverings can reduce viral transmission by 70% if everyone wears them correctly over [their] nose and mouth.Masks are acts of altruism; you are not protecting yourself by wearing a mask, rather, face masks prevent you from infecting others when asymptomatic.

Clean and Disinfect

Cleaning with soap or detergent reduces the amount of germs on surfaces and decreases risk of infection from surfaces. In most cases, cleaning alone removes most virus particles on surfaces. Disinfection to reduce transmission of COVID-19 at home is likely not needed unless someone in your home is sick or COVID-19 positive has been in your home.
